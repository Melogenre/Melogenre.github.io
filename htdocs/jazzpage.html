Jazz Music

Jazz is a musical genre that originates from the African-American community in New Orleans in Louisiana, evolving from ragtime and blues. It is influenced by the folk music of both West Africa and Europe and is often characterized by the musicians’ individual diversity while playing their respective instruments together, making it a complex yet enjoyable type of music to listen to. Jazz music is a way to get into a style of passionate artistic expression flowing freely in the instruments played by the genre’s musicians.

According to the Wikipedia, the term “jazz” came from the word “jasm” which evolved from the word “jism,” an American slang meaning spirit, energy and vigor. According to Herbert Asbury's The Latin Quarter (1938), the term arose in late nineteenth century, when a group of young boys advertised themselves as the “Razzy Dazzy Spasm Band.” They were among the first bands to play what is known to be jazz today. However, another group of musicians claimed their name and musical style. After the original band threw rocks at them, the band name of the imitators was changed to “Razzy Dazzy Jazzy Band.”

The music of jazz is often associated with the sounds of the saxophone, trombone, piano and the double bass. It places a heavy emphasis on improvisation, where the musician improvises over a tune. The genre has distinctive rhythms where they use swings—a technique where a note is lengthened or shortened alternatively. Other jazz styles use syncopated rhythms, where strong beats are weakened and weak beats are emphasized. Jazz also has sophisticated harmonies, making use of extended chords that give more life to a piece.

The beauty of jazz music lies in the way it sounds like freedom. It is able to express a wide range of emotions being experienced in the reality of life, as it was a developed pastime for people who struggled back then. Its strong rhythms give its musicians and people an urge to just groove along to the music being played. Listeners of jazz admire how the flow of the song stays exciting and boisterous, making an adventurous and suave musical experience. Overall, jazz gives the message of focusing on the now and to never stop moving.
