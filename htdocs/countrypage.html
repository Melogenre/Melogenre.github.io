Country Music

Country music, also called country and western, is a type of music originating from the rural areas in South and West America. It became really popular around 1950s to 1960s and is often associated with its local cowboy vibes and its heavy reliance on vocalization and string accompaniments like the guitar and the banjo. The genre evolved from the older genres of blues, bluegrass, and a mixture of various folk and dance music. The influences of Scottish and Irish immigrants also show through the simplicity and sentimentality of country music.

The Country genre, as mentioned previously, uses a lot of string instruments to serve as the accompaniment to the musician’s vocals. Very often, the voice of country music singers have a specific accent and a slight hoarseness to it. Songs under the genre often have sentimental and romantic themes and concepts similar to ballads to tell a story. Group singing is a common type of singing found in country music, especially in early country songs where families and friends would sing together.

Country music in its simplicity allows its listeners to feel a sense of nostalgia, ease and home in their songs. Many people listen to it because they find it to be a small escape from the realities and troubles of life without feeling too overwhelmed. They are also relatable and due to the laid-back nature of the genre, listeners can focus on the message of the song. It’s a relaxing genre that anyone can enjoy.
